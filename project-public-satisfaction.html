Public Satisfaction and Requirement Analysis

Introduction

One of the most emphasized benefits of decentralization is the alleged improvement in public administration by bringing the government closer to the people. In such context, the public officials are expected to have a greater ability to recognize, interpret and satisfy more precisely citizens’ needs and demands for public services. Due to their proximity, citizens can also analyse whether or not their local governments are fulfilling their expectations. If larger population are satisfied with their local officials’ performance, they may express greater support for the system. However, if local institutions are unable to satisfy their demands, citizens will not only express their discontent with local governments, but they can also be increasingly dissatisfied with the way democracy works in the entire nation.

In such case “Public satisfaction and requirements analysis” helps the local and local government to understand the impact of their performance and also identify the needs of their citizen. It further allows the local government to understand how their local service must evolve to meet the changing needs of a contemporary society.

Importance of Research

The Public satisfaction and requirements analysis involves evaluating issues of public importance with the objective of providing facts and statistics about the extent and impact of the various service provided by the local government. The basic objective of this research is to assess the degree to which the local services are meeting their expected goals. Public satisfaction and requirements analysis analyse the impact of current local services and also identifying the requirement or demand of other services.

  • It will help the local government to detect the impact of their provided service and also identify further needs of people which will ultimately help in improving their service
  • On base of the findings of this research, the locality can identify new areas to spend their service budget
  • The finding of this research will mitigate the existing gap between beneficiaries) and service provider
  • The research will let the local government to know whether the provided services are achieving their intended ends or the extent to which alternatives may work better or worse.

Objectives

The overall objective of this research project is understanding the impact of provided local services and other service requirements of citizens and give detail analysis report to the local government. The specific objectives are:

  • To analyse if people are satisfied or not from the services provided by the local government
  • To identify the lapses and areas of improvement in existing service
  • To identify the areas for adding new services by analysing the requirement of people

Outcome

This research project will have following outcome:

  • The local government can assess the performance of its provided services and make improvement accordingly
  • On base of the research data, the government will know about the service needs of people
  • Since this research incorporate the impact analysis of services and requirements of citizens, it can also be used as a preliminary study for planning and allocating the budget that will address the needs of people

Beneficiaries

  • The primary beneficiary is the local government as it will be able to improve and add new services as per the requirement of citizens
  • The people of that locality as their concern of service improvement and requirement will reach to the consideration of local government

Research Process

Following activities are needed to fulfil the objectives:

  • Stage I: Host formal table talk with the representative of local people to identify all the local services and get other required details
  • Stage II: Site inspection and physical geo-mapping of area for sample selection
  • Stage III: Plan data collection methodology and make inclusive research questionaries accordingly with findings in previous stage
  • Stage IV: Mobilize researchers to collect data from specific wards
  • Stage V: Data analysis and report preparation
  • Stage VI: Submit final report to the local government
  • Stage VII: Organize a discussion session between public and people representative
